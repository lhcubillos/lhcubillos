## ðŸ”­ I'm currently working on:
1. [_A deep-learning-augmented Kalman filter for high-performance intracortical decoding_](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1NmY85_TAf4n49pjoc5JUDZaUa91NrVSc&usp=drive_fs): we're studying KalmanNet, an algorithm that enhances the Kalman filter by adding deep learning to learn the measure of trust between physics and neural activity. We are seeing an improved performance in real-time finger movement decoding in primates, which is achieved by allowing the brain to have a lazy computation and only care about change. 
2. [_Compression or noise reduction: the role of dimensionality reduction methods in understanding the brain-muscle relationship_](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qzoZKnchd1lAl9XmRiMehS9bmmAmsGjF/view?usp=sharing): dimensionality reduction methods are widely used to study brain and muscle latent factors, but it is unclear whether these techniques are able to remove noise or are just an efficient way of compressing the data. We have found that, when trying to predict muscle activity from neural activity, dim. reduction methods were able to compress very effectively, but didn't show signs of actually denoising the data.
